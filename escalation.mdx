## When to Escalate

Escalate to NIU after-sales when:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Diagnosis is unclear" icon="circle-question">
    You have followed the diagnostic steps and the root cause is still unknown.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Part not available" icon="box-open">
    The required replacement part is not in stock and the customer cannot wait.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Repair unsuccessful" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    You completed the repair procedure but the fault persists.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Safety concern" icon="shield-exclamation">
    You have identified a safety issue that is outside normal repair scope.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Before You Escalate

Make sure you have completed the following:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Run full diagnosis">
    Use the diagnostic code list and relevant failure diagnosis section for the model.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Document the fault">
    Note the exact error code (if any), symptoms, and conditions under which the fault occurs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the wiki">
    Confirm you have followed the repair procedure for this fault in the wiki.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Gather scooter details">
    Have the model name, serial number, and purchase date ready.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How to Submit a Case

<Note>
  Contact information and case submission form to be added by NIU EU after-sales team.
</Note>

When submitting, include:
- **Model name** and **serial number**
- **Error code** shown on display (if any)
- **Symptoms** — describe what the customer reported and what you observed
- **Steps already taken** — what you diagnosed and attempted
- **Photos or videos** — attach if relevant

## Escalation Response Time

| Priority | Situation | Expected Response |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| High | Safety issue or scooter inoperable | Within 1 business day |
| Normal | Fault persists after repair attempt | Within 2–3 business days |
| Low | Part availability or documentation query | Within 5 business days |
