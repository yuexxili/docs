## Battery Voltages by Model

| Model | Voltage |
|-------|---------|
| NQi (2016–2017), NQi Series, NQi Sharing | 51.4V (or 71.4V dual) |
| NQi Cargo | 51.4V |
| MQi GT, MQi GT EVO, UQi, UQi GT | 54.6V |

## BMS Status Indicators

| Indicator | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| ONLINE | BMS functioning normally |
| OFFLINE | BMS failure — do not continue riding |
| Red flash (LED) | Fault status — check diagnostic codes |
| BMS MOS Failure | MOSFET in BMS has failed |

## Charging Rules

<Warning>
  Always use the original NIU charger. Charging with a non-original charger may cause circuit or battery failure and voids warranty.
</Warning>

## Failure Diagnosis

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Battery not charging">
    1. Check charger with a known-good unit if available
    2. Inspect charging port for damage or corrosion
    3. Check BMS status — OFFLINE indicates BMS fault
    4. Check diagnostic code on display — refer to [Diagnostic Codes](/repair/diagnostic-codes)
    5. If BMS is offline, escalate — do not attempt BMS replacement without authorization
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Battery drains faster than expected">
    1. Check battery age and cycle count (via NIU app if connected)
    2. Check for parasitic drain — inspect all electrical connections
    3. Test battery capacity with diagnostic tool
    4. If capacity is significantly below spec, battery may need replacement — escalate
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="BMS MOS Failure">
    Symptom: Display shows BMS MOS failure code.

    1. Power cycle the scooter
    2. If fault persists, do not charge or ride
    3. [Escalate to after-sales](/escalation) — BMS replacement required
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Battery pack removal (for inspection)">
    Disassembly order: **Section 4 (seat) → Section 20 (motor access) as noted in the service manual.**

    Preparation: Remove Section 4 first before accessing battery compartment on most models.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Red LED flash on battery">
    Red flash indicates fault status.

    1. Note the flash pattern (count of flashes)
    2. Match to diagnostic code list — refer to [Diagnostic Codes](/repair/diagnostic-codes)
    3. Follow the relevant fault procedure
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Battery Safety

- Never short-circuit battery terminals
- Do not expose battery to temperatures above 45°C or below -20°C
- Do not puncture or crush the battery pack
- If battery is swollen, cracked, or leaking — do not charge. Isolate and [escalate immediately](/escalation)

<Note>
  Battery replacement requires NIU authorization. Do not replace battery packs without contacting after-sales first.
</Note>
