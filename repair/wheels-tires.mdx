## Tire Pressures by Model

| Model | Front | Rear |
|-------|-------|------|
| NQi X | 1.7 bar | 2.3 bar |
| Other models | Check model-specific manual | Check model-specific manual |

<Note>
  Always check tire pressure when cold (scooter not ridden for at least 2 hours).
</Note>

## Wheel Components

- Front tire
- Front wheel axle
- Front alloy wheel
- Valve and valve cap
- Front wheel oil seal
- Bearing
- Intermediate spacer
- Brake disc (front)
- Outer spacer

## Inspection Checklist

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check tire pressure">
    Inflate to correct pressure for the model. See table above or model page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check tire tread depth">
    Inspect tread wear indicators. Replace tire if worn to minimum depth.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check tire sidewalls">
    Look for cuts, bulges, or cracks. Replace immediately if found.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check wheel bearings">
    Spin wheel by hand — should rotate smoothly and quietly. Roughness or noise indicates worn bearings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check wheel for damage">
    Inspect alloy rim for cracks, bends, or corrosion.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check valve">
    Ensure valve cap is fitted. Check valve core is not leaking.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Failure Diagnosis

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Flat tire / slow puncture">
    1. Remove wheel
    2. Inspect tire for puncture source
    3. Repair or replace inner tube (if tube-type) or use puncture repair kit (if tubeless)
    4. Check valve — replace valve core if leaking
    5. Reinflate to correct pressure
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Wheel wobble">
    1. Check wheel bearing — replace if rough or noisy
    2. Check wheel axle nut torque — tighten if loose
    3. Check rim for bending — replace rim if bent
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Wheel bearing noise">
    1. Remove wheel
    2. Remove oil seal
    3. Remove and inspect bearing — replace if rough, noisy, or has visible damage
    4. Reinstall with correct intermediate spacer placement
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Wheel Removal (General)

<Warning>
  Support the scooter securely on a stand before removing any wheel.
</Warning>

1. Loosen axle nut
2. Disconnect brake hose or caliper mounting (front only)
3. Remove wheel axle
4. Remove wheel carefully — note spacer positions for reassembly
