## How to Read Diagnostic Codes

Error codes appear on the scooter's dashboard display as a number. Some models also indicate faults via LED flash patterns on the battery pack (red flash = fault status).

<Note>
  Always note the exact code before starting diagnosis. Some codes clear after power cycling — if the code does not reappear, monitor for recurrence before returning the scooter.
</Note>

## Codes by Category

### Motor & Controller (10-13, 110, 111, 120, 190, 191)

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="10 — FOC controller locked rotor (stalled)">
    **Cause:** Motor is mechanically stuck or brakes are applied while throttle is open.

    **Resolution:**
    1. Check for mechanical obstruction in wheel/motor
    2. Check brake lever switches — ensure brakes are fully released
    3. Check FOC-Motor-ECU-Harness connections
    4. If clear, check motor phase lines — see [Motor & Drivetrain](/repair/motor-drivetrain)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="11 — FOC controller under/over-voltage">
    **Cause:** Battery voltage outside safe range for the controller.

    **Resolution:**
    1. Check battery charge level — charge if low
    2. Check battery voltage with multimeter
    3. Check Battery-FOC-ECU-Motor-Harness connections
    4. If battery is within spec and fault persists, suspect FOC failure
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="12 — FOC controller over-current">
    **Cause:** Motor drawing more current than controller limit.

    **Resolution:**
    1. Check for mechanical load on motor (obstruction, seized bearing)
    2. Check FOC-Motor-Harness connections
    3. Allow to cool and retry — if recurs, escalate
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="13 — FOC controller over-temperature">
    **Cause:** Controller temperature exceeded limit.

    **Resolution:**
    1. Stop riding — allow controller to cool (15-30 min)
    2. Check ventilation around FOC area
    3. If fault recurs immediately after cooling, FOC may be failing — [escalate](/escalation)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="110 — FOC/Controller power fault">
    **Cause:** FOC driver power fault — internal power supply failure.

    **Resolution:**
    1. Check FOC-ECU-Dashboard-Harness-Throttle connections
    2. Power cycle scooter
    3. If fault persists, replace FOC controller — [escalate](/escalation)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="111 — FOC verification failed">
    **Cause:** FOC cannot communicate with ECU.

    **Resolution:**
    1. Check FOC-ECU-Dashboard-Harness connections — reseat all
    2. Power cycle
    3. If fault persists — [escalate](/escalation)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="120 — Motor fault / phase line failure">
    **Cause:** Missing motor phase — scooter unable to ride.

    **Resolution:**
    1. Check motor phase wire connections (U, V, W)
    2. Inspect for damaged or disconnected phase wires
    3. If wiring is good, motor windings may be faulty — [escalate](/escalation)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="190 / 191 — Controller communication failure">
    **Cause:** Controller cannot communicate with ECU/Dashboard.

    **Resolution:**
    1. Reseat all ECU and FOC harness connectors
    2. Inspect CAN bus wiring for damage
    3. Power cycle
    4. If fault persists — [escalate](/escalation)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Battery & BMS (22-23, 30-33, 50-51, 80, 121-122, 130-139, 150-158, 165-166, 178, 183, 194)

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="30 — Battery 1 over-charge protection">
    **Cause:** Battery 1 voltage reached over-charge threshold (71.4V on 60V models / 51.5V on 48V models).

    **Resolution:**
    1. Stop charging immediately
    2. Check charger — use only original NIU charger
    3. If BMS failed to cut off charging — [escalate](/escalation)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="130 — Battery 1 over-discharge protection">
    **Cause:** Battery 1 discharged below safe threshold.

    **Resolution:**
    1. Charge battery immediately with original NIU charger
    2. If battery does not recover after full charge — [escalate](/escalation)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="131 — Battery 1 discharge over-current">
    **Cause:** Battery discharging at too high a current.

    **Resolution:**
    1. Check for motor overload — see [Motor & Drivetrain](/repair/motor-drivetrain)
    2. Check throttle for stuck condition
    3. If fault occurs at normal load — [escalate](/escalation)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="132 / 133 — Battery 1 over-heat / low-temperature protection">
    **Cause:** Battery temperature outside safe operating range.

    **Resolution:**
    - **Over-heat (132):** Stop riding, cool battery in shade. Do not charge until cooled.
    - **Low-temp (133):** Warm scooter to above 0°C before riding. Normal in cold weather — advise customer.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="134 — Battery 1 open circuit or imbalance">
    **Cause:** Cell imbalance or open circuit within battery pack.

    **Resolution:**
    1. Power cycle
    2. If fault persists — [escalate](/escalation), battery pack has internal failure
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="135 — Battery 1 short-circuit protection">
    **Cause:** Short circuit detected in battery pack.

    **Resolution:** Do not charge or ride. Isolate battery — [escalate immediately](/escalation)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="136 — Battery 1 water ingress">
    **Cause:** Water detected inside battery pack.

    **Resolution:** Do not charge or ride. Isolate battery — [escalate immediately](/escalation)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="138 — Battery 1 MOS transistor damaged">
    **Cause:** MOSFET in battery BMS has failed.

    **Resolution:** Do not charge or ride — [escalate immediately](/escalation)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="121 — BCS MOSFET damaged">
    **Cause:** MOSFET in Battery Control System has failed.

    **Resolution:** Do not charge or ride — [escalate immediately](/escalation)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Throttle (140-142)

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="140 — Throttle fault">
    **Cause:** Throttle signal abnormal.

    **Resolution:**
    1. Check throttle connector — reseat
    2. Inspect throttle wiring for damage
    3. Replace throttle assembly if faulty
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="141 / 142 — Throttle open/short circuit (NQi X)">
    **Cause:** Open or short circuit in throttle wiring.

    **Resolution:**
    1. Inspect throttle wiring for damage or chafing
    2. Replace throttle assembly
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Connectivity & Locks (60-67, 72, 99, 161-163)

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="99 — Communication bus failure">
    **Cause:** ECU or harness communication failure (highest priority fault).

    **Resolution:**
    1. Reseat all ECU harness connectors
    2. Inspect wiring for damage
    3. Power cycle
    4. If fault persists — [escalate](/escalation)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="60 / 62 / 63 / 64 — SIM / GPS fault (NQi X, NQi Sharing)">
    **Cause:** SIM card or GPS module problem.

    **Resolution:**
    1. Check SIM card seating — replace if damaged
    2. Check GPS antenna connection
    3. If ECU is faulty — [escalate](/escalation)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="161 — Scooter locked remotely">
    **Cause:** Scooter remotely locked via NIU system.

    **Resolution:** Contact NIU after-sales to unlock — [escalate](/escalation)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="162 — Electric faucet lock failure (NQi X)">
    **Cause:** Electric handlebar lock failure. Faucet may not be turned fully left when powering off.

    **Resolution:**
    1. First: update ECU to latest firmware
    2. If fault persists: replace electric faucet lock
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
