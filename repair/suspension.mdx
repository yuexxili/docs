## System Overview

NIU scooters use:
- **Front**: Telescopic fork assembly (left and right shock absorbers with lower yoke plate)
- **Rear**: Rear shock absorber(s)

## Front Fork Components

- Welded lower yoke plate assembly
- Front left shock absorber assembly
- Front right shock absorber assembly
- Bolt M10×1.25
- Lower conical bearing
- Bearing cover
- Lock nut
- Gland nut

## Inspection Checklist

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visual inspection">
    Check fork legs for bends, cracks, or oil leaks. Check rear shock for oil leakage or visible damage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Compression test">
    Push down firmly on handlebars (front) and rear seat (rear). Suspension should compress and rebound smoothly without bottoming out or sticking.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check for play">
    With scooter on stand, check for excessive side-to-side play in front fork. Any play indicates worn bearings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check mounting bolts">
    Verify all suspension mounting bolts are torqued correctly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Failure Diagnosis

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Rear shock absorber failure">
    Symptoms: Excessive bouncing, bottoming out, oil leak from shock body.

    1. Inspect shock absorber for oil leakage — if leaking, replace
    2. Check shock mounting bolts — tighten if loose
    3. Compress shock by hand — if no resistance, shock is failed — replace
    4. Replace shock absorber assembly
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Front fork oil leak">
    1. Clean fork legs and identify exact leak source
    2. Replace fork seals — requires fork disassembly
    3. Inspect fork tube for scratches or pitting — replace tube if damaged
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Steering feels stiff or notchy">
    1. Check steering head bearings — worn or dry bearings cause notchy steering
    2. Lubricate or replace steering head bearings
    3. Check for bent fork or frame damage — escalate if structural damage is found
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Vibration through handlebars">
    1. Check tire balance and pressure — see [Wheels & Tires](/repair/wheels-tires)
    2. Check wheel bearings
    3. Check fork leg tightness and yoke plate bolts
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Warning>
  Do not ride a scooter with a failed shock absorber or damaged front fork. Return to the customer only after repair is confirmed.
</Warning>

<Note>
  If structural damage to the fork or frame is found, [escalate to after-sales](/escalation) before proceeding.
</Note>
